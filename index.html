About Me

About a year ago, I transitioned into tech. Tasked with creating the first user guide for an exciting software startup, I was handed login credentials and little else. Within weeks, I had user guide online and began interacting with clients to better understand what they needed from the software. From there, I forged a relationship with our software engineering team to communicate user feedback and stay up to date on feature additions and UI changes.

My work with the software engineers at work peaked my curiosity. Eager to engage in logic-based, problem solving, I enrolled in Northwestern University's twenty-five week, full stack, coding bootcamp. Through this program I've been able to build out web pages and applications with a variety of programming languages and frameworks, such as HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, Bulma, Express.js, React.js, and Node.js. It also provided me an opportunity to work in a variety of technologies including Heroku, MongoDB, MySQL, Command Line, Database Theory, and GitHub.

When I'm not coding, you can find me training for my next marathon or spending time with my dog, Olive.
